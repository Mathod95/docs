Namespaces

Kubernetes Namespaces are essentially labels that partition a cluster into smaller, distinct segments. They allow you to organize your resources into groups that reflect different projects, teams, or environments within the same cluster. Imagine a cluster as a big office building: without any signs or room numbers, finding the right office would be a challenge. Namespaces act as these signs, guiding you to the right room â the right resource â quickly and efficiently.

  3. Access Control: Namespaces work hand in hand with Kubernetesâ Role-Based Access Control (RBAC) system, allowing administrators to restrict user permissions within specific Namespaces.

Kubernetes starts with four initial Namespaces:
